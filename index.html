How to organize and get started on your project

Read time: 5-10 minutes
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

As students and aspiring journalists, it's important to stay organized and have a clear direction in our projects in school and beyond. I've sometimes struggled trying to know where to start, so I wanted to build a guide that would help people like me to find the right way to start their projects!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Notebook/Scratch Paper
  • Pen/Pencil/Writing Utensil
  • Computer/Tablet/Phone
  • Vision (not literally, but what you want out of this project!)

Now, before we get started, take your sheet/notebook and write your response to each step. Or, if you prefer to use your computer/phone/tablet to type this out, you're more than glad to do so!

  1. Narrow your topic for your project.

    This is sometimes the difficult part, but this is what will assist you in knowing what to do for your projec! The first thing you can do is turn your general idea into a specific topic. This will allow you to not only focus on what you want, but it will contribute more value into the field in relation to your topic.

  2. Set preliminary research goals.

    This idea of writing down what you're looking for is based on what you want to achieve within your research. What is it that you want top prove or show?   3. Assess the overall scope of your project.

    Understand what your scope is when organizing yourproject, it's essentially the boundaries of what you'll be covering. This eliminates the chance of facing irrelevant information in your search.

  4. Identify possible sources.

    Planning out what sources you want to use for your project is important, especially if your topic may need to involve a primary or secondary source. When researching, it's important what kind of source is required to support your project. As aspiring journalists, it's important to have lots of supporting sources, especially those that are considered primary. But, then again, it all depends on what you are writing about! Look around at similar projects and see what sources they're using, and maybe use similar sources if you find what you need!

  5. Brainstorm keywords

    Focus on the words that make up your research topic list specific words that can help narrow or encapsulate findings. This is just an overall good way to guide you throughout your project!

  6. Gather your materials

    So gather everything that'll you'll need to go on and start putting your project in motion.
